# Partner Channel Strategy

**Public page:** `/partners`  
**Priority:** P2 — activate during / after first city pilot

---

## Why partners

National marketing is expensive and untargeted. **Schools, brands, and local networks** already trust barbers — they shorten pilot recruitment and add revenue beyond booking fees.

---

## Partner types

### 1. Barber schools & academies

| | |
|---|---|
| **They get** | Graduate placement, modern booking lab, co-branded “powered by ShopTheBarber” |
| **We get** | Pipeline of solo barbers (ICP-A) and shop hires |
| **Mechanics** | Referral codes, cohort onboarding week, optional curriculum module |
| **Owner** | Founder / growth |

### 2. Product brands (marketplace)

| | |
|---|---|
| **They get** | SKU listing, provider shelf distribution, checkout at booking time |
| **We get** | Marketplace GMV, brand marketing co-spend, retail attach |
| **Mechanics** | `products` + provider marketplace tools; rev-share on fulfilled orders (TBD contract) |
| **Owner** | Ops + marketplace PM |

### 3. Local business networks

| | |
|---|---|
| **They get** | Member benefit (“founding shop” pricing), local case study |
| **We get** | Warm intros to 5–10 pilot shops in one city |
| **Mechanics** | Chamber events, equipment vendor bundles, pilot city MOU |
| **Owner** | Founder / city lead |

---

## Sequencing

```
Pilot city (5–10 shops)
    → 1 school MOU (student barbers)
    → 1–2 brand SKUs in marketplace
    → Network/chamber announcement
    → Case study → next city
```

Do **not** sign national brand exclusives before pilot ROI is proven.
